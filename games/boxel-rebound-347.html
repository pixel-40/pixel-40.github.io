How to play: Start with a simple goal—survive longer, finish a round, or complete an objective—then build consistency. As you get comfortable, look for small advantages like better angles, smarter spacing, or cleaner execution.

Tips for better results: Use small, controlled movements rather than rushing—consistency usually beats speed. If you’re stuck, take a breath, reset your approach, and focus on one improvement at a time. Headphones can help you notice audio cues, but the game is still fully playable muted.

Why people keep coming back: Short rounds make it easy to practice, and the core loop encourages you to beat your own personal best. Whether you’re aiming for a clean run, a faster time, or a higher score, improvements feel noticeable from session to session.

Good to know: If you encounter slowdowns, try closing extra tabs, switching to another browser, or lowering quality settings (when available). For the best experience, keep the game window focused and avoid heavy downloads while playing.
